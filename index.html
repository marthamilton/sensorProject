About This Project

Many parents and teaching professionals are not aware of the threat posed by air pollution around schools and the possible long-term effects. The aim of this project is to utilise computer hardware and data visualisation to provide parents, governors, teachers, local MPs and the general public with an up to date and accessible way of understanding the air quality in and around schools.

This project involves creating a forward thinking proof of concept, with the long term aim of it being maintained and extended. A sensor will be placed at a school location to collect live data which will be displayed using 3D mapping and its location. The sensor will be purchased and setup for data to be transmitted securely over the internet and then stored in a database. The data provided by the sensor will be compared to environmental standards with consideration of local surroundings and its contribution to air pollution. This data will be made available to the public using an interactive website displaying the data visualisation and analysis. The interactive website will present the data so that it is accessible to all. Being based on a 3D map featuring the location of the sensor will allow for more sensors to be added at other school locations in the future.

In addition to creating the proof of concept, the project will also involve exploring the wider context of such a system, the benefits of having this information and its influence in society as a whole. The school involved will be part of any decision making and be continually updated with progress throughout the project.
